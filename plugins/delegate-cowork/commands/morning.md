# Morning

Prepare a morning brief. Be direct and concise.

## Workspace Discovery
Scan the mounted workspace for context files:
- delegation.md (Claude's task queue)
- tasks.md (user's tasks)
- roadmap.md (upcoming milestones)
- scratch/ (working notes)
- archive/ (completed items)

Use what exists. Skip what doesn't. Don't create scaffolding.

## Check What's Available

**Google Calendar MCP:** Try listing today's events via the Google Calendar connector. If the connector isn't available, note it and move on.

**Gmail MCP:** Try listing recent emails via the Gmail connector. If the connector isn't available, note it and move on.

If neither connector is available, build the brief from workspace files alone and suggest enabling Calendar and Gmail connectors for a richer brief.

## Gather Context

**Always read:**
- `tasks.md` - what's active, what's next
- `roadmap.md` - upcoming deadlines

**If Calendar connector available:**
- Pull today's events
- Note meetings, who's involved, prep needed

**If Gmail connector available:**
- Pull recent emails (last 24 hours)
- Focus on: flagged/urgent emails, emails from people mentioned in tasks

**Recent activity:**
- Check `archive/` for recently completed items
- Scan `scratch/` for recent working notes

## What to Surface

1. **State of things** - What got done, what's in motion, patterns worth noting

2. **Today's focus** - One or two things that matter most. Not a task list - the priority.

3. **Blockers** - Anything stuck, anything being avoided, decisions needed

4. **People** - Who to reach out to, follow up with, or prepare for (from calendar + email)

## Style

- Brutally honest, not cheerful
- Surface what might be missing or avoided
- If something should be cut or delegated, say so
- Have a point of view, don't just summarize
